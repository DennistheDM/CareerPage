Research

Master's Thesis · TU München · 2025

"How does Cloud Computing support Strategic Entrepreneurship in Firms?"

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Dr. Holger Patzelt
Chair: Entrepreneurship, TUM School of Management
Method: Qualitative research — semi-structured interviews
Scope: 6 industry practitioners across 6 countries
Submitted: February 2025
View Full Thesis ↗

This research investigates how cloud computing technology facilitates strategic entrepreneurship within firms — examining whether it enables organizations to simultaneously preserve existing competitive advantages while pursuing new business opportunities. Using a qualitative approach with six senior practitioners across cybersecurity, banking, software, manufacturing, and IT services, the study produces a three-cycle coding framework revealing how cloud-derived advantages translate into entrepreneurial capabilities.

Cloud Computing Strategic Entrepreneurship Value Creation Opportunity-Seeking Competitive Advantage Qualitative Research ATLAS.TI

Key Findings

01

Cloud computing directly strengthens an entrepreneurial mindset by enabling affordable experimentation and continuous cloud-based learning.

02

It fosters entrepreneurial culture by lowering market entry barriers, enhancing agility, and reducing the cost of failure.

03

Cloud-driven leadership support enables data-driven decision-making and faster, more collaborative strategic choices.

04

Strategic resource management is enhanced through scalability, pay-per-use models, and focus on core business competencies.
